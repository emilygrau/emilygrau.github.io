About Me

Hi there, my name is Emily. I am a third-year student at Georgia Tech, pursuing a B.S. of Computer Engineering. I had gone into my first year of college as a future civil engineer, but after taking my first coding class, I found my passion. By the end of the semester I officially switched my major to computer engineering and never looked back. You, like many others, may ask why CmpE and not CS? To that I say, knowing how things work at their core has always intereseted me. In an age where the future is technology, I think it's important to understand how the devices that are changing the world truly work.

Now, as a future computer engineer, I am adept in both high-level and low-level languages. In the hardware realm, I have experience with circuitry and embedded devices. Everyday I am coding and doing my best to advance in the languages I already know and love, as well as learning new languages to add to my repertoire. Whether it be through school or on my own, I am always looking for experiences that will allow me to grow as a computer engineer and a Girl Who Codes.
